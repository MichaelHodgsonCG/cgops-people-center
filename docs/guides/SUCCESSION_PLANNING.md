# Succession Planning in People Center — Guide

> For HQ and executives. Succession planning lives in **Bench & Risk** and
> is visible to executives and admins only.

## Why it exists

CG's growth is gated by leadership readiness, not capital or sites. The
succession board answers, at a glance: *if this seat opened tomorrow, who
steps in — and how deep is the line behind them?*

## The concepts

- **Seat** — a key position at one place: "General Manager — Beertown
  Waterloo" or "Chef de Cuisine — Sole". One seat per position per
  location (a seat can also be regional, e.g. a regional leader seat).
- **Incumbent** — who holds the seat today. A seat with no incumbent is a
  vacancy you're planning for (e.g. an upcoming location).
- **Successors** — the ranked list of people who could take the seat.
  Rank #1 is your first call. People from any location can be listed —
  succession is where cross-location moves get planned.
- **Coverage** — computed automatically from the successor list, never
  typed: **red** = no successors, **yellow** = one deep, **green** = two or
  more deep. When readiness assessments ship (Phase 3), coverage will also
  weigh *how ready* each successor is, not just how many there are.

## How to use it

1. Open **Bench & Risk** → *Succession planning* → **Plan a seat**.
2. Pick the position and location, and the incumbent if the seat is
   filled today.
3. Add successors with **add successor** — each lands at the next rank.
   Remove and re-add to re-order.
4. Watch the KPI card **"Succession seats < 2 deep"** — the goal is to
   drive it to zero for key seats.

A practical starting point: create the 16 General Manager seats, then the
16 Chef de Cuisine seats. Even one honest successor per seat immediately
shows where the bench is thin.

## Privacy rules (important)

- **Executive altitude only.** Regional leaders, GMs, and below cannot see
  succession — not the seats, not the candidates, not their own standing.
  This is enforced in the database, not just hidden in the interface.
- **Nothing leaks onto timelines.** Adding someone as a successor never
  appears on any person's timeline or profile. All succession changes are
  recorded in the audit log for accountability.
- **People never see their own standing** — being (or not being) on a
  successor list is HQ knowledge until HQ chooses to have that
  conversation.

## What it feeds

- The **Bench & Risk KPIs** today (thin seats, vacancy risk).
- With Phase 3 (readiness assessments): coverage graduates from "how many
  successors" to "how many *ready* successors", and the readiness
  distribution joins the dashboard.
- With Phase 5 (CGOPS integration): a bench-strength summary flows into
  the executive Company Briefing — as business signal only, never names
  attached to notes.

## Good practice

- A successor list is a plan, not a promise — keep it honest, not
  aspirational.
- Pair every yellow/red seat with development action: the successor's next
  step belongs in their development plan (Phase 3) or a development note
  today.
- Review seats quarterly; incumbents and lists go stale silently.
